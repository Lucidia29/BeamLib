What Batch 4 adds. 2D Timoshenko beam with the same DOF order as EB2D [ux, uz, θy]. Shear-deformable kinematics with constitutive shear stiffness κGA. Locking-free Φ-corrected closed-form 2-node stiffness: defining Φ = 12 EI / (κGA L²), the bending/shear block has a (1+Φ) denominator and (4±Φ)L² coupling entries. Reduces exactly to the EB2D Hermite block in the slender limit Φ → 0. Consistent mass = Hermite translational mass (same as EB2D) plus explicit rotary inertia ρ·Iz·L/6 · [[2,1],[1,2]] on the θy DOFs. The Timoshenko–EB2D mass delta is exactly this 2×2 rotary block.

Why deep beams need shear

Why this is the key test. The cantilever and slender-limit tests could in principle pass on a Timoshenko element with a sign error in the shear term — Φ being small enough that the bending term still dominates. This deep-beam test pins the shear-flexibility magnitude to 4 significant figures of agreement (ratio 1.04875), making any subtle constitutive error visible at the 1e−9 level.

The relative difference equals the analytical shear-fraction prediction to 3 significant figures, confirming both Timoshenko and EB are solving the correct problems and that Timoshenko is approaching the EB result by exactly the amount it should.

Convergence picture

Slender limit asymptotics.

For a tip-loaded cantilever, the relative shear contribution is

  ushear / ubend  =  3 E I / (κ G A L²)

For a rectangular cross-section with κ = 5/6, ν = 0.3:

  = 0.65 / κ · (h/L)²  =  0.78 · (h/L)²

At L/h = 1000: 0.78 × 10⁻⁶ — exactly the measured relative difference.

Why this matters

Shear locking in two-node Timoshenko elements.

Naive low-order C⁰ interpolation (linear uz, linear θy) produces an element whose spurious shear strain energy grows as κGA/L². For very slender beams the genuine bending energy ∼ EI/L³ becomes negligible compared to the spurious shear term, so the deflection collapses by orders of magnitude.

The Φ-corrected closed-form element uses the exact homogeneous-solution shape functions of the Timoshenko equations. These continuously interpolate the EB Hermite functions in the slender limit and produce no spurious shear energy. With 4 elements at L/h = 1000 the result is correct to floating-point precision.

Verified locking-free.

Why no EAS or SRI. Enhanced assumed strain (EAS) and selective reduced integration (SRI) are alternative locking-free techniques requiring extra implementation machinery and care around mode counts. The Φ-corrected closed form has no extra DOFs, no quadrature subtleties, and produces the analytical answer exactly in the slender limit — appropriate for a teaching-oriented Phase 1 library.

Φ-corrected vs EB bending block

Timoshenko (general Φ):

  Kb = EI / (L³(1+Φ)) ·

  [ 12    6L   −12   6L    ]

  [ 6L   (4+Φ)L² −6L (2−Φ)L² ]

  [−12  −6L   12   −6L    ]

  [ 6L   (2−Φ)L² −6L (4+Φ)L² ]

EB limit (Φ → 0):

  Kb = EI / L³ · [12, 6L, −12, 6L; 6L, 4L², −6L, 2L²; …]

Identical to EB2D's Hermite block.

Mass-matrix strategy. Timoshenko 2D uses consistent translational Hermite mass (same as EB2D) plus explicit rotary inertia ρ·Iz·L/6 · [[2,1],[1,2]] on θy via linear shape functions. PROJECT_SPEC §3.4 requires rotary inertia for Timoshenko mass; without it any modal / dynamic analysis would miss the rotational kinetic energy contribution. The fully-consistent Friedman–Kosmatka Φ-dependent mass is more elaborate and is deliberately deferred to a later batch — for slender-to-moderate beams the rotary-inertia delta is the dominant Timoshenko correction.
